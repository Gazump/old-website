What is Counselling?

Talking about things can be very powerful. Often in life, when we are trying to resolve a problem or work through a difficulty, talking to someone we trust helps us to overcome these challenges. But what happens when we can't (or don't want to) speak to someone close to us? What if talking to a friend or family member could make things worse?

Counselling and therapy offer an excellent solution to this problem. In a session, your counsellor follows strict rules of confidentiality, but more importantly, offers a safe space to talk about absolutely anything. They are trained to help guide you through your own thoughts and feelings in a way that empowers you to find the positive change in your life that you are looking for. It is important to realize that a counsellor won't be able to give you the answers, but can offer you ways to discover those answers for yourself. You are the expert on your own life and a counsellor is there to support you in your mastery.

How does counselling work?

Sessions take place face-to-face in Oakham, or online via video/voice through Skype, Zoom or Teams.

A session lasts for 50mins and would normally occur weekly or fortnightly for a minimum of 6 weeks.

In your first session, you will simply chat with your counsellor as you both get to know each other and build a relationship of trust. You will discuss what has brought you to counselling and the counsellor will form an assessment and offer their ideas on the best way forward for you in therapy. Your counsellor will discuss with you all aspects of confidentiality and answer any questions you might have about counselling.

If both you and your counsellor decide to pursue therapy, you will sign a contract and agree dates/times for future sessions.

It is important that you and your counsellor are a good fit, and if this is not the case, they will refer you on to someone who would be better.
